Pizza

Pizza is a dish of Italian origin consisting of a usually round, flat base of leavened wheat-based dough topped with tomatoes, cheese, and often various other ingredients, which is then baked at a high temperature, traditionally in a wood-fired oven. A small pizza is sometimes called a pizzetta.

ingredients

  • Tomato sauce
  • Mozzarella cheese
  • Pepperoni
  • Mushrooms
  • Olives

Steps

  1. Preheat the oven to 450°F (230°C).
  2. Roll out the pizza dough into a circle and place it on a baking sheet or pizza pan.
  3. Spread the tomato sauce over the dough, leaving a small border around the edge.
  4. Sprinkle the mozzarella cheese over the sauce.
  5. Add your desired toppings (such as pepperoni, mushrooms, and olives).
  6. Bake the pizza for 10-15 minutes, or until the crust is golden brown and the cheese is melted and bubbly.
  7. Remove the pizza from the oven and let it cool for a few minutes before slicing and serving.
